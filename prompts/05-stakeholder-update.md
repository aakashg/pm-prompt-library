# Stakeholder Update

**Category:** Communication
**Use when:** You need to write a weekly update, status report, or executive summary fast.

## The Prompt

```
You are a PM writing a stakeholder update. Make it scannable, honest, and action-oriented.

Write a status update following this structure:

**TL;DR** (2 sentences max — the update if they read nothing else)

**Status: 🟢 On Track / 🟡 At Risk / 🔴 Blocked** (pick one)

**Progress This Week**
- [Shipped/completed items — be specific, link to PRs or docs if relevant]

**Next Week**
- [What's planned — with owners if applicable]

**Risks & Blockers**
- [Active risks with mitigation plans]
- [Blockers with who/what is needed to unblock]

**Decisions Needed**
- [Any decisions you need from the reader — be specific about what you need and by when]

**Metrics Update** (if applicable)
- [Key metric]: [current value] vs [target] — [trend: up/down/flat]

Rules:
- Lead with what matters. No throat-clearing.
- If there's bad news, put it above the fold, not buried at the bottom.
- Every risk needs a mitigation. Every blocker needs a next step.
- Keep the whole thing under 200 words. Executives don't read long updates.

Raw notes/context:
[PASTE YOUR MESSY NOTES, SLACK THREADS, MEETING NOTES, OR BULLET POINTS]

Project: [PROJECT NAME]
Audience: [WHO IS READING THIS — CEO, VP Eng, cross-functional team, etc.]
```

## Tips for Better Results

- Paste raw Slack threads or meeting notes — the messier the input, the more time this saves
- Specify the audience — an update for your CEO vs. your eng lead should read differently
- Include last week's update so the AI can highlight what changed
